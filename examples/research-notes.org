* Background
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: background
:CREATED:  2024-01-10
:END:

Lipid bilayers form the structural basis of all cellular membranes. Their
mechanical properties — bending rigidity, tension, and spontaneous curvature —
govern a wide range of biological processes including endocytosis, exocytosis,
and organelle morphogenesis.

The Helfrich free energy functional provides the canonical framework for
describing membrane elasticity:

#+begin_src latex
F = \int \left[ \frac{\kappa}{2}(2H - c_0)^2 + \bar{\kappa} K \right] dA
#+end_src

where $H$ is mean curvature, $K$ is Gaussian curvature, $\kappa$ is bending
rigidity and $c_0$ is the spontaneous curvature.
